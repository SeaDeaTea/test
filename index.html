Do's and Don'ts

Do

  • Turn me on my side

  • Call my parent when it's safe

Don't

  • Put anything in my mouth
  • Restrain my movements
  • Give me food or water
  • Leave me alone

What is Dravet Syndrome?

A quick explanation.

Dravet Syndrome is a rare, lifelong form of epilepsy that begins in the first year of life. It's caused by a mutation in the SCN1A gene, which affects how the brain regulates electrical activity.

Seizures in Dravet can be triggered by fever, heat, excitement, or they can happen with no warning at all. They can look different each time — some involve convulsions, others are more subtle.

I am not in constant danger. I live a full life. But when a seizure happens, knowing what to do makes a real difference.
